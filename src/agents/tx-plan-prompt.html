Your Treatment Plan for Strep Throat

Strep throat is a bacterial infection that causes inflammation and pain in the throat. It is caused by the Streptococcus pyogenes bacteria. Symptoms may include sore throat, difficulty swallowing, fever, and swollen lymph nodes.

Health Professional Referrals

  • Primary Care: Strep throat is typically diagnosed and treated by primary care providers such as family physicians and pediatricians.
  • Specialists: In most cases, a specialist referral is not necessary for the treatment of strep throat. However, if complications arise or if the infection does not respond to treatment, an ear, nose, and throat (ENT) specialist may be consulted.

Tests to Confirm Condition

  • Laboratory Tests:
    • A throat culture is the most accurate test for diagnosing strep throat. A swab is taken from the back of the throat and sent to a laboratory to check for the presence of Streptococcus pyogenes bacteria.
    • Rapid Strep Test A rapid strep test can provide quick results, but it is less accurate than a throat culture. A swab is taken from the back of the throat and tested for the presence of strep bacteria.

Treatments

  • Prescription Medication Options Antibiotics, such as penicillin or amoxicillin, are the primary treatment for strep throat. They help kill the bacteria causing the infection and reduce the duration and severity of symptoms.
